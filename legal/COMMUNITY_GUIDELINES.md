# MatchMind Community Guidelines & Platform Handbook

**Last updated:** [EFFECTIVE_DATE]
**Applies to:** every MatchMind user — students, coaches, and parents — across all features (profiles, scout notes, shared to-do lists, AI chat, and any messaging features).

> MatchMind exists to help robotics teams prepare, scout, and compete at their best. Most of our users are students, many of them minors — so the bar for behavior here is the same as at an event: **respectful, honest, and safe.** These guidelines are part of our [Terms of Service](./TERMS_OF_SERVICE.md); breaking them can lead to content removal, suspension, or a ban.

> **Reminder:** MatchMind is an independent app with **no affiliation** with VEX Robotics, Innovation First International, the REC Foundation, or RobotEvents. Data and AI answers in the app are unofficial — event officials and official publications are always the authority.

## 1. The short version

1. **Be respectful.** No harassment, hate, bullying, threats, or humiliation — of any person or any team.
2. **Keep it appropriate.** No sexual, graphic, violent, or shocking content. Language filters are enforced; don't try to evade them.
3. **Protect privacy — yours and others'.** Don't share anyone's personal information. Don't post photos of people without permission.
4. **Compete with integrity.** Scout fairly, don't sabotage, don't cheat, and follow the rules and codes of conduct of the events you attend.
5. **Be honest about who you are.** Real role, real team, one account, no impersonation.
6. **Use AI responsibly.** Verify rules and important decisions with official sources.
7. **Report problems.** See something unsafe or against these rules? Report it in-app or email [CONTACT_EMAIL].

## 2. Respectful conduct

- Treat every user the way competition expects teams to treat each other — courteously, even when you disagree or compete head-to-head.
- Zero tolerance for: harassment or bullying; hate speech or slurs of any kind; threats or intimidation; sexual content or advances (this is a youth platform); doxxing; encouragement of self-harm.
- Trash talk about a *robot's design* is part of robotics culture; attacks on *people or teams* are not. Critique designs, not humans.
- Don't spam, flood chats, mass-promote, or send repetitive unsolicited content.

## 3. Safety for minors (this is non-negotiable)

- Never ask another user for personal contact information, social media handles, addresses, school schedules, or photos.
- Any tournament-wide chat is intentionally limited: users appear by **first name and team number only**, chats exist only around the event window, and messages pass through automated language filtering. Do not attempt to work around these protections.
- Adults (coaches/parents) must keep interactions with students team-appropriate and competition-focused.
- If anyone makes you uncomfortable, **block and report them** — and tell a coach, parent, or trusted adult.
- We cooperate with lawful requests from authorities on safety matters involving minors.

## 4. Privacy and other people's content

- Post only content you have the right to post. Get permission before uploading identifiable photos or videos of other people — especially other teams' members.
- Scout notes can include observations about robots and match performance. Keep them factual and competition-relevant; do not record personal information about students (faces are fine to avoid; never names + personal details).
- Don't publish private team strategy that was shared with you in confidence.

## 5. Competition integrity

- **Scouting** means observing public matches and public information — that's fair game and what MatchMind is built for.
- It does **not** mean: accessing other teams' private data, deceiving teams to extract strategy, interfering with robots or equipment, or manipulating match data.
- Don't use MatchMind to violate any rule of the events you attend, including the codes of conduct of the organizations that run them. If MatchMind data or AI output ever conflicts with what officials say — **officials win, full stop.**
- Alliance discussions and offers through the app must be honest. Don't make offers you don't intend to honor, and don't impersonate another team in negotiations.

## 6. Honest accounts and roles

- One person, one account. Choose your real role — student, coach, or parent — and your real team(s). Misrepresenting a team affiliation or a role (e.g., an unrelated adult posing as a coach) is grounds for an immediate ban.
- You're responsible for what happens on your account. Keep your sign-in secure and don't share it.

## 7. Content rules for specific features

- **Scout notes & attachments:** competition-relevant photos/videos only (robots, fields, matches). No inappropriate or off-topic media.
- **Shared to-do lists:** keep tasks and comments team-appropriate; the same conduct rules apply.
- **AI chat:** don't try to make the AI produce hateful, sexual, dangerous, or rule-breaking content; don't submit other people's personal information; remember AI answers are unofficial.
- **Any messaging features:** automated profanity/abuse filtering is enforced (including common abbreviations and evasions). Filtered messages don't send. Repeated attempts to evade filters are a violation on their own.

## 8. Enforcement

We use a combination of automated filtering and human review of reports. Depending on severity and history, we may:
1. **Warn** you and/or remove the content;
2. **Restrict** features (e.g., chat or sharing);
3. **Suspend** your account temporarily;
4. **Ban** your account permanently and, where appropriate for safety, notify your coach/organization or authorities.

Severe violations (threats, sexual content involving minors, doxxing, hate speech) skip straight to suspension or ban. We may preserve and disclose content as required by law.

**Appeals:** if you believe we got it wrong, email [CONTACT_EMAIL] from your account email within 14 days. A human will review.

## 9. Reporting

- Use the in-app report/block options where available, or email **[CONTACT_EMAIL]** with screenshots and the user/team involved.
- Reports are confidential. Retaliating against someone for reporting is itself a violation.
- For emergencies or immediate danger, contact local authorities first.

## 10. Platform handbook — using MatchMind well

A few norms that make the app better for everyone:

- **Data freshness:** competition data comes from the public RobotEvents API and may lag the venue. Treat in-app schedules/scores as helpful, not authoritative — check event displays and announcements.
- **Predictions are estimates.** Match probability bars and alliance suggestions are statistical tools to support — never replace — your team's judgment.
- **Source-check the AI.** The assistant cites sources where possible; click them. For rules questions, the official game manual and Q&A are the only authority.
- **Good scouting hygiene:** record notes right after matches, rate consistently, attach a photo when it helps, and share with your team so everyone benefits.
- **Be a good alliance citizen:** respond to offers respectfully — a polite decline costs nothing and keeps the community healthy.

## 11. Changes

We may update these guidelines as features evolve (e.g., if messaging or tournament chats launch or change). Material changes will be announced in-app. Continued use means acceptance.
